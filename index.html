The transformative patent-pending design of filterNgo assists the use of face masks. Our device provides a flow of filtered air and creates a healthy environment inside the face masks.

The filterNgo device's ability to cool the air inside the face mask makes it ideal for using it during work and strenuous activities.

Benefits of using filterNgo.

Maintain acceptable levels of carbon dioxide and oxygen to keep the body and mind healthy.

Reduce pollution AQI index. In large cities, the AQI is above the acceptable index value of 50 points on most days.

Reduce humidity resulting in better skin.

Achieve air circulation up to 82 liters per minute which is adjustable as activity level increases.

The inflow of cooler air keeps the body at an operating temperature.

Comfort for up to standard 6 hours of operation. Additional 6 hours for essential employees.

Changeable filters that switch to fit the job requirements.

The filterNgo Team.

The filterNgo was invented in December of 2020 by Galymjohn Kuras. Founder and integral member of filterNgo, LLC’s technical team, Galymjohn Kuras is an engineer with experience in design, product development, customer discovery, and manufacturing. Working in conjunction with Purdue University Northwest’s Commercialization & Manufacturing Excellence Center, the filterNgo team is working to bring this product to market.

The key members of filterNgo are:

Mont Handley, Ph.D. The commercial Sales Officer will lead the commercial efforts and discussions. Mont is associate director of the Commercialization and Manufacturing center at Purdue Northwest with decades of experience driving small business innovation, research, and development.

Ildar F Akhmadullin, Ph.D. a Technical Research Officer, Purdue Northwest University engineering professor with a research and teaching focus on heat transfer and energy conversion in automation and industrial applications.

John J. Durocher Ph.D. Biological Sciences-Exercise Physiology. John has done extensive work in the fields of applied human physiology and exercise physiology. Much of his research has involved the study of how the sympathetic division of the autonomic nervous system regulates arterial blood pressure at rest and during a variety of stressors.
